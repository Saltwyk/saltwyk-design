Observations

  • Emerald (A): Consistent with the interactive color system. Selected = clickable. May imply "action" when selection is just data.
  • Indigo (B): Creates clear separation: emerald = action buttons, indigo = selection state. Still colorful and visible.
  • Slate (C): Most neutral option. Selection is pure information, no color meaning. May feel less "designed".
  • Ink (D): Maximum contrast, works well in both modes when inverted. No semantic color meaning.

Key Question: If emerald means "interactive/clickable," should selected checkboxes/radios/toggles use emerald? They ARE interactive, but the emerald communicates "click me" more than "I am selected."
