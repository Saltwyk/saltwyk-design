Disqualification Handling

When a merchant selects an unsupported platform, the experience should feel informative, not rejecting. Key principles:

Tone

  • Warm, not cold: Use orange/warning tones, not red/error
  • Explain why: Give context (Shopify app approval process)
  • Offer value: Waitlist keeps them engaged, not abandoned
  • Provide exit: Clear path to leave without frustration

What Not to Do

  • Don't use error states (red, X icons)
  • Don't say "sorry" excessivelyâ€”be matter-of-fact
  • Don't leave them stranded with no next action
  • Don't hide the limitation until after they've invested time

Edge Cases

Storeleads Data is Wrong

If the merchant indicates data is incorrect, the confirmation card switches to edit mode. All fields become editable inputs. After saving, the agent confirms the updated values before proceeding.

Storeleads Data Not Found

If no data is found for the store URL, skip the confirmation card and present an inline form to collect: Store URL, Monthly Revenue estimate, and Product Category. This should be rare for established stores.

Merchant Wants to Switch Platforms

If a merchant starts with Shopify but wants to try with a different platform, they can use "Save & Exit" and restart. The system should not auto-save the Shopify selection until they explicitly join the waitlist.

Multiple Stores

For merchants with multiple stores on different platforms, they should complete onboarding for each store separately. This is handled at the account level, not within Section 1.
