Campaign Strategy

Coordinated launch campaigns provide significant advantages over individual launches.

Why Campaigns Matter

  • Network effects: More merchants launching together = more immediate value for shoppers
  • Marketing coordination: Shared promotional resources and cross-promotion
  • Strategy guidance: Each campaign comes with a strategy video and playbook
  • Community support: Merchants help each other succeed in the same cohort

Campaign Structure

Element Purpose
Theme Unifies marketing messaging across all merchants
Launch Date Coordinated go-live for maximum impact
Cohort Size (10-20) Large enough for network effects, small enough for community
Strategy Video Campaign-specific guidance and best practices
Recommended Balance Based on expected volume for that season

Network Effects

The value of rewards commerce grows exponentially with each new merchant and shopper.

How It Works

  • More merchants = more places for shoppers to earn and spend points
  • More shoppers = more rewards transactions at each merchant
  • More transactions = faster clearing balance turnover
  • Coordinated launches = immediate critical mass of network activity

Campaign vs Individual Launch

Metric Individual Launch Campaign Launch
Day 1 Network Partners Variable (depends on existing network) 10-20 guaranteed
Marketing Support Self-directed Coordinated, shared resources
Time to First Transaction Days to weeks Often same day
Community None Cohort Slack channel, peer support

Exclusion Philosophy

Exclusions are a power user feature. Most merchants don't need them.

When to Use Exclusions

  • Direct competitors: A camping store might exclude another camping store in the same market
  • Brand conflicts: A luxury brand might exclude discount retailers
  • Value conflicts: A vegan brand might exclude leather goods sellers

When NOT to Use Exclusions

  • Just because they're similar: Complementary products often drive cross-sales
  • Preemptively: Wait to see if there's actual friction before excluding
  • Broadly: Excluding many merchants reduces network value

Key Messaging

"Most merchants don't use this feature" - sets expectation that full network access is normal and valuable.

Edge Cases

Campaign Fills Up

If a campaign reaches capacity while merchant is selecting, show "Campaign Full" state with option to join waitlist or select different campaign. Pre-validate availability on card display.

Merchant Changes Mind on Campaign

After selection but before terms, allow "Change Campaign" link. After terms acceptance, changes require contacting support (to prevent gaming of spots).

No Campaigns Available

If all campaigns are full or none exist, only show "Go Live Now" option. Explain that campaigns will return and they can join later from dashboard.

Exclusion Search Returns No Results

If merchant searches for a merchant not in the network, explain that merchant isn't on Saltwyk yet. Offer to notify them when merchant joins.

Terms Rejection

If merchant won't accept terms, they cannot complete setup. Save & Exit preserves progress. Offer to connect with support if they have questions about terms.

Post-Setup: What Happens Next

Verification (1-2 Business Days)

  • Automated checks on business information
  • Stripe account verification
  • Rewards platform connection validation
  • Merchant receives email when complete

Funding (After Verification)

  • Merchant logs into dashboard
  • Initiates ACH transfer for clearing balance
  • Funds arrive in 1-2 business days
  • Auto-fund enabled if configured

Go Live

  • Immediate: Once funded, merchant is live within 24 hours
  • Campaign: Merchant goes live on campaign launch date with cohort

Ongoing Support

  • Campaign merchants: Cohort Slack channel, strategy sessions
  • All merchants: Dashboard, email support, phone support
