Full expressive range—where the brand shines brightest.

Design Philosophy

Marketing has permission to be expressive. While product UI exercises restraint to keep users focused, marketing earns its drama. The goal is to make prospects feel the brand before they understand it—to sense that Saltwyk represents something different, substantial, trustworthy.

Both opal treatments are available. Fire opal (bright threads on dark intaglio) commands attention in hero sections, footers, and "wow" moments. Bright opal (soft thread washes on ledger paper) builds trust in feature explanations, pricing sections, and forms. The interplay creates rhythm—dark for impact, light for comprehension.

Sepia intaglio illustrations are the signature visual element—currency engraving aesthetics that communicate "this is real money infrastructure, not another fintech startup." Thread lines at 100% saturation act as sparks of energy against dark surfaces. Gradient text makes headlines memorable. Motion and scroll behaviors reward engagement without distracting from the message.

Remember the brand promise: "the payment network that helps merchants prosper." Every marketing composition should feel like it was built by people who take money seriously—not earnest startup energy, but the quiet confidence of established infrastructure.

When to Use Which Opal

Fire Opal

  • • Hero sections (page top)
  • • Footer and pre-footer CTAs
  • • Testimonial blocks
  • • "Wow" moments and reveals
  • • Dark social proof sections

Bright Opal

  • • Feature explanations
  • • Pricing sections
  • • Trust-building content
  • • Forms and inputs
  • • FAQ and support content

Rhythm guidance: Alternate between fire and bright opal as you scroll. Never two dark sections in a row. Light sections can repeat for extended content. Thread lines mark major transitions.
