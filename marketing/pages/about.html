About Saltwyk

Points are hard to redeem. Saltwyk makes them easy to spend.

[Illustration: Salt market metaphor]

A historic trading scene reimagined with modern currency elements - salt crystals transforming into points flowing between merchants and shoppers

Shoppers earn billions of points every year.

Points are hard to redeem. Saltwyk makes them easy to spend.

Every expired point is a sale that didn't happen, because spending points was never easy enough. For merchants, that's customers who had a reason to buy and didn't.

For shoppers, it's fatigue. Rewards that feel like work without pay. Points expiring before they're useful. People stop believing in merchant rewards, and credit cards win by default. Not better, just slightly less broken.

Paying with points feels different. No debt, no bill coming, no anxiety. Just spending what you've already earned. It's lighter.

The infrastructure we have today wasn't built for this. Extractive, legacy payments tech designed for a different era. We think commerce deserves something better.

The name? Saltwyk is an old word for a salt market, a trading town where people exchanged their salt (the original salary) for the things they needed. We're building that for points.

The trillion point economy. When points are easy to spend, everyone wins.
