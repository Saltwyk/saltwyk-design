Page Specification

About Page

Bold declarative approach. Five core belief statements that build narrative momentum. Typography-first — no illustrations. Alternating backgrounds create rhythm. Gradient text on the infrastructure statement. Builds toward "the trillion point economy."

Manifesto Design Decisions

  • Five declarations: Each section makes one bold statement. Short explanations (2-3 sentences). The headlines carry the page.
  • Alternating backgrounds: Spectrum → White → Fresh → Ledger → Dark emerald. Background changes mark declaration boundaries.
  • Gradient text: Only used once — "The infrastructure didn't exist." (fresh gradient). Restraint makes it powerful.
  • Dark emerald CTA: Same gradient as homepage hero. Diagonal line texture. Eyebrow → headline → subhead → button structure.

Let's Talk

Points should be spendable.

Not redeemable. Spendable. Everywhere. The infrastructure to make this possible didn't exist. We're building it.

Points are better than money for everyday transactions.

For shoppers: No debt. No bill coming. No anxiety. Just spending what you've already earned.

For merchants: When you shift your perspective on what points are for, new growth opportunities emerge.

The infrastructure didn't exist.

Legacy payments tech was built for the finance industry to extract value. Points needed something better.

The points economy should help people and merchants prosper.

When points are easy to spend, shoppers spend more and merchants grow without discounting. Infrastructure should serve them, not extract from them.

We're building it.

The infrastructure. The products. The network. From every angle, we're creating the foundation for a points economy where everyone prospers.

The name? Saltwyk is an old word for a salt market — a trading town where people spent their salt (the original salary) for the things they needed. We're building that for points.

We're accepting early merchants and partners

The trillion point economy.

Early merchants shape the network. Early partners shape what we build. Both lock in economics that won't be available at scale.
